# GTM Positioning: Scope Tracker

## Market Context

50,000+ law firms operate in the US with fewer than 50 attorneys. 60%+ do some fixed-fee work. Most have no dedicated IT. Legal tech adoption is notoriously slow—many firms still track time and scope with paper or spreadsheets.

The core problem: Fixed-fee work is economically dangerous when scope drift is invisible. A project estimated at 40 hours but taking 65 hours is a silent profit killer. Most managing partners know this happens but treat it as "the nature of the work" rather than a solvable problem.

Market opportunity: If a firm discovers they're overrunning by 30-50% on fixed-fee projects, the ROI on tooling is immediate.

## Competitive Positioning

**vs. Clio (full practice management)**
- Clio: Comprehensive, powerful, expensive ($400-900/month), requires training
- Us: Single-purpose (scope tracking), $199/month, works like email

**vs. LeanLaw (billing-focused)**
- LeanLaw: Tracks billing, doesn't detect scope drift as it happens
- Us: Real-time scope tracking; alerts when project approaches budget

**vs. Spreadsheets (the real competitor)**
- Spreadsheets: Free, familiar, manual, no automation, no real-time alerts
- Us: The answer is not to eliminate spreadsheets—it's to make them unnecessary

**Positioning Statement**: "Scope tracking that works like email, not like software."

## Target Buyer

**Decision Maker**: Managing partner at a 10-30 person transactional law firm

**Firm Profile**:
- Fixed-fee work: 40-70% of revenue
- Has experienced budget overruns on fixed-fee matters
- Attributes overruns to "the nature of the work" (not a process problem)
- Uses spreadsheets or paper for time tracking
- Wants visibility without complexity

**Buying Trigger**: The moment a partner realizes they're overrunning on a high-profile matter and can't easily quantify why.

## The "Aha Moment"

Show the partner their actual overrun data from the past 6 months.

Every firm we've talked to underestimates scope drift by 40-60%. Partners guess "we're probably over by 10-15 hours per matter" when the data shows 35-50 hours.

Quantified overrun data is the sales tool. Once they see it, they can't unsee it.

## Sales Motion

**Zero-Touch Free Trial**
- No sales call. No setup meeting. Partner downloads, enters 1-2 past matters, runs analysis.
- First matter analysis is free and shows overrun data.

**Single Matter Proof**
- Partner runs Scope Tracker on 1 active deal
- Real-time alerts when matter approaches budget
- Second month: Partner sees whether alerts prevented overrun

**Firm Rollout**
- If first matter shows value, partner rolls out to team
- Adoption from managing partner down is natural (not bottom-up)

**Partner-to-Partner Referral**
- If it works, the managing partner tells another managing partner at a peer firm
- This is the growth engine. If referral rate is healthy, product-market fit is real.

**The Product IS the Sales Motion**
- If Scope Tracker solves the problem, partners talk about it
- If it doesn't, no amount of sales will fix it
- Success metric: referral rate >20% of new firms

## Pricing Strategy

**Flat Fee Structure**: $199/month per firm (not per-seat)
- Law firms hate per-seat pricing (encourages hoarding)
- Flat fee aligns incentive: more attorneys using it = better ROI for the firm

**Annual Contract**: Paid annually with first month free
- First month free removes activation energy
- Annual lock-in reduces churn (firms that see ROI in Month 2 stay)
- ROI payback: typically Week 1-2 (first scope drift prevented pays for the tool)

**No Negotiation**
- $199/month is the price. Not negotiable for SMBs.
- Protects unit economics and prevents feature creep (no custom builds)

## Target Buyer Segments

**Segment 1: High-Volume Fixed-Fee Firms**
- 50%+ of revenue from fixed-fee work
- Monthly volume: 15+ active matters
- Pain: Portfolio-level overrun tracking is manual

**Segment 2: Mixed-Fee Firms with Profitability Pressure**
- 30-50% fixed-fee work
- Pressure to improve margins (partner compensation at risk)
- Pain: Can't isolate which practices are unprofitable

**Segment 3: Am Law 200 Practices Testing Alternative Fee Arrangements**
- Large firms experimenting with fixed-fee on specific practice areas
- Buying trigger: Need to prove AFAs are viable before rolling out firm-wide

## Channel Strategy

**Bar Association Events**
- Not booths; hosted sessions on "managing fixed-fee profitability"
- Partner panels: "How we reduced scope drift by 25%" (external credibility)
- Sponsor CLE credits on fixed-fee risk management

**Legal Operations Conferences**
- Audience: Operations directors and CFOs at mid-size firms
- Message: Scope drift kills profitability; here's how to measure it

**Managing Partner Peer Networks**
- Law firm peer groups (Vistage, peer councils)
- Partner introductions and referral partnerships
- Word-of-mouth in these networks moves fast

**Content Marketing**
- "The $X cost of scope drift" (white paper with real data)
- Case studies: "This firm recovered $500K in overrun" (anonymized)
- Email campaigns targeting managing partners at SMB firms

## Key Metrics

**Primary Metric**: Partner-to-partner referral rate
- If >20% of new firms come from referrals, product-market fit is real
- If <10%, product isn't solving the problem well enough

**Secondary Metrics**:
- Free trial conversion rate (% of free users who become paid)
- Churn rate (should be <5% annual; low because first-month ROI is high)
- NRR (net revenue retention; should be >100% for healthy SMB product)
- Matter-level ROI tracking (do partners see the money prevented by alerts?)

## Lessons from Early Conversations

- Partners already know scope drift is a problem; they don't know how to measure it
- Most firms have a spreadsheet but don't use it consistently (too manual)
- The selling point is not "beautiful UX"—it's "clear overrun data"
- Legal tech adoption is slow *except* when the ROI is immediate and obvious
